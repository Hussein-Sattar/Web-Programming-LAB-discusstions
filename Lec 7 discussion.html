1. What are the generic font families? Explain in details with examples

Generic font families in CSS are keywords that are used to specify a broad category or type of font.
         These families are more general and provide a fallback in case the specific font specified is not available on the user's system. 
         The five generic font families are:

         Serif, Sans-Serif, Monospace, Cursive and Fantasy.
